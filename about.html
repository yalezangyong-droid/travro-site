Brand Manifesto

For twenty-five years, we made luggage for the brands you know. We learned what breaks first and what never breaks. We learned that a good wheel can save a trip and a bad zipper can ruin one. We learned that the gap between a $50 suitcase and a $500 suitcase isn't always $450 worth of engineering.

So we decided to skip the markup.

TRAVRO is luggage by the people who actually make luggage. Designed alongside the people who know how a polycarbonate shell flexes under pressure, how a telescoping handle should lock, and how many times a Hinomoto wheel can spin before it gives up (answer: it doesn't).

We believe good design should be accessible. We believe engineering should be transparent. We believe the carry-on you take to Tokyo and the one you take to your parents' house for Thanksgiving should both make you feel like you have your life together.

Travel in your own light. TRAVRO.
How It Started

The factory that finally built its own brand.

For 25 years, a factory in Jiangxi, China has been the silent force behind luggage brands you already know. They didn't build a brand. They built expertise — one hinge, one wheel, one zipper at a time.

TRAVRO is what happens when that expertise finally gets its own name.

We're not a brand that outsources manufacturing. We are the manufacturer.

That's why we can offer Hinomoto wheels, YKK zippers, and TSA-approved locks at a price point that doesn't require a coupon code to feel fair.

Design Philosophy

Three rules. No exceptions.

Every TRAVRO product follows three rules:

  • One-tone system.

    Shell, handle, wheels, zipper — all the same color. No visual noise. This isn't a design choice; it's a design discipline.

  • Engineering-grade components.

    Hinomoto wheels (Japan), YKK zippers, TSA-approved combination locks. These are the same component specifications found in luggage that retails for twice our price.

  • Quiet confidence.

    We don't make the loudest suitcase at the carousel. We make the one you trust to show up intact after 50 flights.

The Founder

Built by someone who knows both sides.

TRAVRO started with a gap that most people never see.

On one side: a factory in Jiangxi, China, with 25 years of engineering mastery — the kind of place that makes luggage for brands you already own, then watches someone else's logo go on the product.

On the other side: American travelers paying $300 for carry-ons built with the exact same components.

Will Zang saw both sides. He'd spent a decade at Maersk — the world's largest shipping company — building markets across three continents. He studied at Yale. He understood manufacturing from the inside. He understood what American consumers actually value. And he kept coming back to the same question: why does great luggage engineering always hide behind someone else's name?

TRAVRO is the answer. Not a brand that found a factory. A factory that finally built its own brand — with a team that knows both worlds well enough to bridge them.

Will Zang is the co-founder of TRAVRO, a design-forward luggage brand built in partnership with a factory that spent 25 years making luggage for the world's biggest brands. Before TRAVRO, Will spent a decade at Maersk — the world's largest shipping company — where he rose from entry-level to senior management in eight years, building markets across West and Central Africa and leading major corporate integration projects. He holds a Master of Advanced Management from Yale School of Management, where he was the first Chinese citizen admitted from the University of Ghana. He has lived and worked across China, Africa, and the United States. Based in California.
